Kayseri Manti Recipe

Kayseri Manti is a traditional Turkish dish consisting of small dumplings filled with spiced meat, typically served with a garlic yogurt sauce and a drizzle of melted butter infused with sumac.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water (more if needed)
  • 200g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon sumac (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead until you form a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. In another bowl, mix the ground meat, chopped onion, cumin, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Roll out the dough on a floured surface until it's very thin, about 1/16 inch thick. Cut the dough into small squares, approximately 1.5 inches on each side.
  4. Place a small amount of the meat mixture in the center of each square. Fold the dough over to form a triangle or rectangle, and press the edges to seal. You can also bring the corners together to form a small pouch.
  5. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the manti and cook for about 8-10 minutes, or until they float to the surface and are cooked through. Drain and set aside.
  6. In a small bowl, mix the yogurt with the minced garlic. Season with a pinch of salt.
  7. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, remove from heat and stir in the sumac if using.
  8. To serve, place the cooked manti on a plate, drizzle with the garlic yogurt sauce, and then pour the melted butter over the top.
  9. Garnish with additional sumac or chopped parsley if desired. Serve warm.
